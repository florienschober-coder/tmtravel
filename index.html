Our Story

From Sustainability to Regenerative Tourism

At TM Travel Family Journeys, we believe that exploring the world shouldn't cost the earth — but we also know that simply being 'sustainable' is no longer enough. We are committed to Regenerative Tourism: actively designing trips that leave a destination better, healthier, and more resilient than we found it.

Why? It started on a family trip to Italy. Heavy backpacks, exhausted kids, and the constant stress of piecing together a 'conscious' holiday. We felt more like sherpas than parents. When we finally reached a quiet campsite far from the tourist masses, it clicked: the owners of small local markets were losing out to all-inclusive giants, while well-meaning parents like us were too drained to seek them out. We weren't the only ones. So many families want to show their kids the world — #NatureConnection — but they also just need a real holiday. That is why we started this.

We are allergic to greenwashing. We rely on facts, not fluffy promises — and exclusively partner with accommodations and guides holding recognized eco-labels such as Travelife or Green Destinations.

No greenwashing. No lugging tent pegs. Just the space to truly be together, knowing your presence leaves the place better than you found it.

What We Stand For
Our Three Pillars

Every decision at TM Travel runs through three non-negotiable commitments. These are not slogans — they are our operating system.

Low-Carbon Travel

We exclusively use train travel — not because it looks good, but because the data is clear: rail emits about 5 times less CO² per passenger kilometre than flying. We never compromise on this. #TrainTravelEurope

Local-First Economy

Every euro you spend stays in the destination. We work exclusively with locally-owned accommodations, independent guides, and family-run suppliers — preventing economic leakage to multinational chains.

Regenerative Impact

We go beyond neutral — and that word "beyond" is one we take seriously. Most travel companies stop at carbon neutrality: calculate the damage, pay someone to offset it, done. We think that misses the point entirely. #SustainableVacation

What it means

We don't ask "how do we avoid damage?" — we ask "how do we make it better?" Every trip leaves measurable improvements behind.

In practice

Our CO²-Positive Upgrade funds Rewilding Europe's verified, publicly reported projects. One family trip plants up to 40 trees in the Velebit mountains.

For your kids

Forest tracking, sapling planting, wildlife spotting — your children don't watch conservation, they are part of it.

The Numbers Don't Lie
The Impact of Your Choice

Why #SlowTravel is the Ultimate Secret to Family Bonding in Europe

By TM Travel Team • Reading time: 3 mins

Have you ever returned from a family vacation feeling like you need another holiday just to recover? As modern professionals juggling demanding careers, decision fatigue is real. Rushing from one famous landmark to the next, managing exhausted children, and trying to stick to a rigid schedule can quickly drain the joy out of exploring new places. That is exactly why we champion #SlowTravel for families, especially those travelling with children in that magical 6 to 11 age group.

Slow travel is more than a trend. Researchers Dickinson and Lumsdon (2010) describe it as a fundamentally different approach to tourism, one built on shorter distances, lower-carbon choices, and a far greater emphasis on the travel experience itself. More recently, Manthiou et al. (2022) found that slow travel is about making conscious choices — choosing deceleration over speed so that the journey itself becomes a moment of connection rather than a stressful transfer between home and destination. For families, this shift is genuinely transformative.

When you choose to slow down and commit to just one beautiful destination, the real magic begins. Imagine replacing the chaos of airport security with a relaxing #TrainTravelEurope experience. Whether your train winds through the Italian Alps or glides towards the Croatian coast, your family is not simply moving from point A to point B. You are actively sharing a moment. Card games, local snacks, and real conversation replace the frantic scroll through departure boards. The journey itself becomes the first memory of the trip.

#FamilyFriendlyTravel is not about seeing everything; it is about experiencing things deeply and with genuine curiosity. Research increasingly shows that parents seek immersive encounters with local communities specifically for the educational benefit of their children (ScienceDirect, 2024). Choose Italy, and slow travel means learning the place-based art of making pasta alongside a local family. It means sitting in a sun-warmed piazza, watching your children bravely order gelato in broken Italian. These are the moments where true intercultural understanding takes root — where children begin to see the world not as a backdrop for photos, but as a living community of which they are briefly, meaningfully, a part.

Perhaps you choose Slovenia for your adventure. Here, our #SustainableVacation philosophy centres on #NatureConnection. Around Lake Bled, children can run freely, paddle in crystal waters, and breathe clean alpine air. Slovenian communities have long held a deep cultural relationship with their natural environment, and engaging with that tradition teaches children something no classroom ever could: that caring for a landscape is itself a form of cultural heritage. A simple hike becomes a lesson in both environmental respect and global citizenship.

If you opt for the layered history of Croatia, our #TravelWithKids approach tailors each exploration to curious minds. Walking the ancient walls of Dubrovnik becomes an interactive journey through centuries of Adriatic culture. Learning about local rewilding projects gives children a tangible sense of contribution — a powerful feeling that their #EcoFriendlyHoliday leaves a positive mark rather than a footprint.

Curating these rich #CulturalExperiences without a frantic pace allows your family to genuinely reconnect. A fully planned, low-stress itinerary lifts the logistical burden entirely off your shoulders. You are free to laugh, marvel at unexpected details, and build a legacy of memories that your children will carry long after the tan fades.

At TM Travel, we believe a holiday should never be a checklist. By embracing a slower, more deliberate way to explore a single destination, you give your children the world — and you give yourself the time to truly enjoy it alongside them.
